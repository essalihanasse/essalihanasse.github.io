I am a PhD candidate in Computer Science at **Université Paris-Saclay** (DAVID laboratory, UVSQ), employed by **Institut VEDECOM**, under the supervision of Prof. Mustapha Lebbah. My thesis develops generative AI methods to optimize, supervise, and intelligently prepare ADAS validation tests with end-to-end proof of effectiveness.

My research combines **variational inference**, **latent variable models**, and **statistical learning** to build representative and interpretable latent representations of driving scenarios, reducing the volume of simulation-based testing required for autonomous vehicle safety certification.

Research interests
======
* Generative models (VAEs, structured latent priors, co-clustering)
* Variational inference and MCMC methods
* Optimization and operations research
* Statistical validation and representative sampling
* Quantitative finance

Background
======
Before my PhD, I completed an MSc in Data Science at **Institut Polytechnique de Paris** and an engineering degree at **EMINES – UM6P** (Industrial Management, minor in Data Science). My past work spans VAE-based representative sampling of autonomous driving data at VEDECOM and time series forecasting with stochastic optimization for agricultural planning.
